Research focus

About

My research sits at the intersection of food economics, consumer behavior, and public policy. I focus on how households allocate both money and time to food, and how those choices are shaped by economic conditions, market structure, technology, and policy.

Originally from Bolivia, I bring an applied and policy-oriented perspective to research questions about measurement, access, affordability, and consumer well-being. My work emphasizes clear evidence that can be used by decision makers, researchers, and the public.

Measuring the food system

Improving food expenditure data through new methods, data integration, and expanded geographic detail.

Understanding consumer behavior

Studying eating patterns, grocery shopping, food preparation, and time-use decisions across populations.

Market structure and policy

Analyzing food retail concentration, online grocery adoption, and market changes during economic shocks.
